About Me

The Law-Tech Intersection

The legal system is being rewritten by technologies it does not yet fully understand. I work at that fault line — treating AI not as a tool, but as a jurisdictional problem that challenges how law defines responsibility, rights, and control.

My training spans law (LL.B) and data science (BS) as a single framework for understanding algorithmic systems and how legal systems must respond to them. This approach has taken me across legal environments in the USA, UAE, India, and Pakistan.

I've built and scaled a legal platform connecting professionals across jurisdictions, published work on criminal justice and legal accessibility, and I'm focused on how AI governance is evolving from comparative regulatory models to the deeper question of how law adapts when decision-making is no longer purely human.
