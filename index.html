About Me

The legal system is being rewritten by technologies it doesn't yet fully understand. I work at that fault line — treating AI not as a tool, but as a jurisdictional problem that challenges how law defines responsibility, rights, and control.

My background spans law (LL.B) and data science — not as separate tracks, but as a single framework for understanding algorithmic systems and how legal structures must respond to them. That combination has taken me across legal environments in the USA, UAE, India, and Pakistan.

I've built and scaled a legal knowledge platform, published a book for early-career law students, and spent time doing real legal work — drafting documents, assisting on active cases, and researching AI policy for international organizations. I care about making law clearer, more accessible, and better prepared for what's coming.
