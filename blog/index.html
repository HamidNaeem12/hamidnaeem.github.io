Not a blog.

When I started, there was nothing. No guide, no senior to ask, no roadmap for someone trying to sit at the intersection of law, data, and AI in Pakistan. I failed at things. I figured them out the hard way.

These pages exist so you don't have to. Not as a shortcut — the hard way still teaches things no guide can. But as proof that the path exists, and some notes from someone who's on it.

"The hard way teaches you things no guide ever could. But you still deserve a guide."

— What I wish someone had told me at the start

A note on coverage

The Global South isn't an afterthought here — it's the angle.

Most AI governance writing comes from Brussels, London, or Washington. This toolkit is built from the opposite direction: from someone practicing in Islamabad, working across South Asian and Gulf legal frameworks, and asking what the EU AI Act actually means when the regulator is the FIA, not the ICO.

Essay — How I Got Here

Navigating law, AI, and tech — without a roadmap

An honest account of building at this intersection in Pakistan, where the intersection barely exists yet. No seniors to ask. No alumni to cold-email. No obvious sequence of steps. What I figured out, where I made mistakes, and what I'd tell someone starting from a similar position.

I'm writing this not because I've arrived anywhere, but because the early stages of this kind of journey are the hardest to find honest accounts of.
